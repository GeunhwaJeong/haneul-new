The collective ideation that the term tokenomics encompasses includes a wide range of concepts that define the science and behavior of blockchain economies. In basic terms, tokenomics are the financial foundation of blockchains. Much the same way a building with a poor foundation is doomed to fail, a blockchain without a well-researched, extensively planned, and painstakingly implemented token economy eventually crumbles.

Haneul tokenomics are based on sound financial concepts informed by extensive blockchain research. Designed for scale, the Haneul tokenomics structure is designed to support the financial needs of web3 now and into the future. 

## The Haneul economy

Three main types of participants characterize the Haneul economy:

- **Users** submit transactions to the Haneul platform to create, mutate, and transfer digital assets or interact with more sophisticated applications enabled by smart contracts, interoperability, and composability.
- **HANEUL token holders** have the option of staking their tokens to validators and participating in the proof-of-stake mechanism. HANEUL owners also hold the rights to participate in Haneul governance.
- **Validators** manage transaction processing and execution on the Haneul platform.


## Core components

The Haneul economy is composed of five core components:

- **[HANEUL](./tokenomics/haneul-coin.mdx):** The HANEUL token is the Haneul platform native asset.
- **[Gas fees](./tokenomics/gas-in-haneul.mdx):** Gas fees are charged on all network operations and used to reward participants of the proof-of-stake mechanism and prevent spam and denial-of-service attacks.
- **[Storage fund](./tokenomics/storage-fund.mdx):** The Haneul storage fund is used to shift stake rewards across time and compensate future validators for storage costs of previously stored on-chain data.
- **[Proof-of-stake](./tokenomics/proof-of-stake.mdx):** The delegated proof-of-stake mechanism is used to select, incentivize, and reward honest behavior by Haneul Validators and the HANEUL owners that stake with them.
- **Voting:** On-chain voting is used for governance and protocol upgrades.
